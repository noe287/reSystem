Below is the explanation of how to switch on and off different debugging options: 

  

enum 

{ DEBUG_HELLO = 0, DEBUG_RREP, DEBUG_RREQ, DEBUG_RNOT, DEBUG_ROUTE_TABLE, DEBUG_NETFILTER, DEBUG_SKB, DEBUG_RTSKB, DEBUG_PROC, DEBUG_LOCK, DEBUG_SEQ, DEBUG_METRIC, DEBUG_AUTOLEARN, DEBUG_DISCOVERY, DEBUG_AODV, DEBUG_VERBOSE, DEBUG_DEV, DEBUG_PKTBUFFER, DEBUG_PATHSWITCH, DEBUG_PBINEVENTS, DEBUG_PBINFWD, DEBUG_PBINDECISION, DEBUG_PBINBC, } 

  

; 

  

In order to activate a certain debug option you need to execute the command: 

  

echo debug 0x??? > /dev/air_mesh_rt 

  

where "0x???" is determined by counting the debug enum values from "1" (not 0) mapping the result to a binary representation of hex and then taking its hex equivalent and writing this to the air_mesh_rt table. 

  

For example, In order to activate HELLO, ROUTE_TABLE, and AODV debug options you need to do the following: 

  

DEBUG_HELLO is at location 1 of the bit sequence which is "0001" (we take 4 bits as we are trying to find out hex values). And hex equivalent is 0x1. 

  

DEBUG_ROUTE_TABLE is at location 5. which is "0001 0000" and hex equivalent is 0x10. 

  

DEBUG_AODV is at location 15 which is "0100 0000 0000 0000" and hex equivalent is 0x4000. 

  

Combining them all we will get: 

0100 0000 0001 0001 which is 0x4011 

  

For our case it may be interesting to see RREQ, RREP, ROUTE_TABLE, DISCOVERY, AODV, but activating them all will flood to many info. It may be better to activate them on a step by step manner and when appropriate.
